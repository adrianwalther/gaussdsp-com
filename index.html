How it works

Ordered chaos.
Not random noise.

Most randomizers throw effects at the wall. Gauss / Chaos applies signal flow theory. What sounds like chaos follows rules you'd use yourself — you just wouldn't have thought of that combination.

01 — Tier ordering
Character before modulation. Always.
Saturation and dirt effects precede modulation. Modulation precedes space. Reverb is always last. The chain is random. The logic is not.
02 — Weighted probability
Not all effects are equally likely.
Each effect carries a probability weight. Adjacency rules prevent combinations that would only produce noise — Chorus into Phaser, BitCrusher into Tube Saturation.
03 — Per-effect bypass
Keep what works. Remove what doesn't.
Any effect in the active chain can be bypassed independently. Amber pilot light shows status. The chain continues around it.
04 — Destroy
A hard clipper on the output.
The VU needle tells you when you've gone too far. Drive the output into hard clipping. Watch the meter peg. You'll go too far. That's fine.
